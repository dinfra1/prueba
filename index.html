CULTURE

South Korea shares its traditional culture with North Korea, but the two Koreas have developed different contemporary forms of culture.

Korean art is strongly influenced by Buddhism and Confucianism.

The architecture of pre-modern Korea can be divided into two main styles: that used in the structures of palaces and temples and that used in the houses of common people, which has local variations.

South Korea leads among OECD countries with the most people graduating in science, technology, engineering or mathematics.
